Lost & Found

A p5.js generative artwork inspired by interior spaces, framed landscapes, and warm reflected light.

Description

A room with kitchen and a balcony. Outside the balcony, there are mountains covered in snow. Trees downstairs and bicycles aligned next to each other. In the middle of the room there are tables and chairs, there are three sides of windows, each window reveals a different scenery. The right hand side - faraway snow mountain, middle - trees and bicycles, left - the mountain this building is on (closer scenery). In contrast, I interpreted the image in a more simplified, atmospheric way. Instead of focusing on the full inventory of objects and scenes, I distilled it down to a mood: ‚ÄúSunsetting Kitchen with window.‚Äù My interpretation emphasizes a singular emotional moment, while my partner‚Äôs language emphasized multiple layers of detail. When I translated the image into code, I relied on abstracted methods such as lineworks and overlays. This reduced the complexity of the original description even further, condensing the scene into visual techniques rather than representational detail. The kitchen, the mountains, and the bicycles were no longer distinguishable as individual objects; instead, they became part of a coded pattern and visual system. Across the stages, the image progressively became simpler and more abstract. My partner began with a descriptive narrative that captured the fullness of the scene, while my interpretation stripped it down into a poetic phrase. The coding stage then reduced the image even further, removing recognizable detail in favor of method and style. This shift demonstrates how interpretation often narrows from the richness of lived observation to the essentialized language of design. What begins as an abundance of detail is ultimately distilled into a minimal, abstract outcome.
