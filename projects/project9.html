Experimental AI Terminal: Bias-based Identification System

01. Concept Proposal

Stereotyping prejudice fantasy dance RPG that utilizes a camera to analyze your face, hair, and clothing color to assign in-game attributes based on blatant biases and stereotypes. The core mechanic relies on blatant stereotypes: wearing glasses automatically classifies the player as a Mage, while the absence of glasses assigns them to other roles. Furthermore, the system categorizes players based on perceived gender, assigning those recognized as male to the Tanker role and those recognized as female to the Dealer (DPS) role. To highlight the inherent bias of the camera, the game explicitly voices its "prejudices" through dialogue, such as, "I see you're wearing glasses; you must lack physical strength." Utilizing the loadPixels() function, the sketch generates a dynamic 'Aura' around the player that matches the color of their clothing—for instance, black clothing manifests as a dark-attribute aura. The UI further dehumanizes the player by displaying floating labels over their head, such as 'Class Name', 'Threat Level', and 'Estimated Intelligence'. The gameplay follows the rhythm-based mechanics of Pump It Up or DDR, with arrows and pose icons flowing across the bottom of the screen. Each class requires specific "Skill Poses" to trigger abilities, such as a "pretending to read a book" pose for Mages or a "muscle flexing" pose for Tankers. These poses must be performed in a precise sequence as the difficulty scales with increasing speed and enemy density.
